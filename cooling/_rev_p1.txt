Abstract 7 
Escalating power densities in electronic systems have rendered conventional cooling 8 
technologies increasingly inadequate, motivating renewed interest in two -phase immersion 9 
cooling and other high -heat-flux thermal -management strategies. As silicon remains the 10 
dominant material platform for electronic devices, understanding pool boiling on engineered 11 
silicon surfaces is essential for the thermal design of next-generation electronic cooling systems. 12 
This review exami nes pool boiling on silicon substrates, emphasizing surface -engineering 13 
strategies spanning nanostructured, microstructured, hierarchical, and hybrid-wettability designs. 14 
By analyzing boiling behavior through a unified near -field/far-field framework specif ic to 15 
engineered silicon surfaces, the review distinguishes surface-controlled interfacial mechanisms, 16 
including nucleation, capillary-driven liquid replenishment, and microlayer stability, from larger-17 
scale vapor-transport constraints that ultimately limit CHF. Water is considered primarily as a 18 
benchmark fluid for elucidating the underlying boiling physics on silicon surfaces, whereas 19 
dielectric fluids are discussed for their direct relevance to immersion-cooling applications. Cross-20 
scale analysis shows that, for high-surface-tension fluids, CHF enhancement converges toward a 21 
common upper bound, indicating that surface engineering governs proximity to a hydrodynamic 22 
ceiling rather than redefining the absolute heat-removal limit of the heated component. In contrast, 23 
for low -surface-tension dielectric fluids, CHF decreases systematically with increasing 24 
normalized capillary parameter 𝐵𝑜∗ , indicating that vapor-removal constraints dominate the 25 
boiling crisis and limit the effectiveness of capillary -based surface strategies. Finally, future 26 
research directions are outlined, emphasizing advanced diagnostics, cross -scale experimental 27 
databases, and reliability-oriented data-driven frameworks for the predictive design of silicon-28 
based boiling surfaces for high-power electronic cooling. 29
